# Business Formation Domain Plan

## V1 Scope

V1 focuses on California solo founders who are starting like Edward:

- One individual founder.
- No cofounder in the initial solo tech business.
- Starts as a sole proprietor under their own legal name.
- Gets a local business license first.
- May accept early payments through Stripe as a sole proprietor.
- Avoids DBA/FBN unless using a business name different from their personal legal name.
- Forms a California LLC later when revenue/risk justifies it.

The first named path is for Whale Tales Labs:

- Phase 0: Edward sole proprietor.
- Phase 1: `Whale Tales Labs LLC`.

## Phase 0: Sole Proprietor Validation Mode

Goal:

- Let a founder start lean without forming a California LLC too early.

User workflow:

- Enter legal name.
- Enter city/county.
- Enter whether operating from home.
- Enter public product names.
- Enter whether using a separate business name.
- Enter whether accepting payments.
- Enter whether selling physical goods.
- Enter whether collecting user data.
- Enter whether hiring contractors.

Edward-specific defaults:

- Legal operator: Edward Lee.
- City: Irvine, California.
- Products: MOMO, Trovr, other software/digital products.
- Business name: no separate public business name during Phase 0.
- Stripe: sole proprietor with SSN if early payments are accepted.
- DBA/FBN: not recommended unless operating under a name different from Edward's personal legal name.
- Whale Tales Labs: do not use publicly as the seller/operator unless DBA/FBN is filed first or the LLC is formed.

StartupFiles public footer during Phase 0:

- Use `© 2026 Edward Lee. StartupFiles.`
- Terms and privacy should identify Edward Lee as the operator.
- Do not use `StartupFiles by Whale Tales Labs` during Phase 0.
- Do not use `StartupFiles is a product of Whale Tales Labs LLC` until the LLC exists.

Phase 0 tasks:

- Get Irvine business license under legal name.
- Decide whether a separate business bank account is needed.
- Set up Stripe as sole proprietor if accepting payments.
- Publish terms and privacy before collecting meaningful user data or payments.
- Track revenue/expenses by product.
- Buy Whale Tales Labs domain if available, but mark as personal asset.
- Do not treat domain purchase as formation, DBA, trademark clearance, or liability protection.
- Check seller's permit if selling tangible goods.
- Avoid hardware/preorders until risk justifies LLC and product compliance review.

Phase 0 generated documents:

- Business profile summary.
- Sole proprietor startup checklist.
- Irvine business license prep worksheet.
- Stripe sole proprietor setup worksheet.
- Product-line bookkeeping worksheet.
- Terms/privacy readiness checklist.
- DBA/FBN decision memo.
- Domain ownership record.

## Phase 1: California LLC Formation

Goal:

- Form `Whale Tales Labs LLC` when the business has enough revenue, risk, or seriousness to justify the California LLC cost.

Triggers:

- Customer payments become real.
- Contracts need to be signed.
- Contractors are hired.
- User data risk becomes meaningful.
- Advertiser money starts.
- Rewards/payouts start.
- Hardware, preorders, manufacturing, or physical goods become realistic.
- The founder wants liability separation and cleaner operations enough to pay the annual cost.

Phase 1 tasks:

- Search name availability for `Whale Tales Labs LLC`.
- Confirm registered agent and addresses.
- Prepare California LLC formation worksheet.
- File California Articles of Organization online through SOS/BizFile.
- File initial Statement of Information within 90 days.
- Apply for EIN through IRS.
- Open LLC bank account.
- Move Stripe/vendor accounts to LLC where possible.
- Assign IP from founder personally to LLC.
- Transfer or assign Whale Tales Labs domain to LLC.
- Update public footer/legal operator language.
- Update terms/privacy.
- Create compliance calendar.

Phase 1 generated documents:

- LLC formation intake worksheet.
- Articles of Organization prep worksheet.
- Statement of Information prep worksheet.
- EIN application prep worksheet.
- Operating agreement draft outline.
- Founder IP assignment draft outline.
- Domain transfer/assignment memo.
- Stripe/vendor migration checklist.
- Bank account opening checklist.
- Compliance calendar export.

## Online Form Walkthroughs

The app should not auto-file government forms in V1. It should provide guided walkthroughs:

- What site to open.
- What account/login is needed.
- What information to have ready.
- Which choices match the user's answers.
- What to download/save after submission.
- What to do next.

Walkthroughs needed:

- Irvine business license application.
- California SOS/BizFile LLC Articles of Organization.
- California SOS Statement of Information.
- IRS EIN application.
- CDTFA seller's permit if applicable.
- County DBA/FBN if applicable.

Each walkthrough should include:

- Prerequisites.
- Estimated time.
- Official link.
- Step-by-step guide.
- "Do not continue if" warnings.
- Fields to copy from the user's profile.
- Output/receipt to save.
- Next task unlocked.

## Important Warnings

Global disclaimer:

- The app is not a law firm, CPA firm, or tax advisor.
- The app prepares documents and guides users through official resources, but users are responsible for verifying, filing, and getting professional review where needed.

Specific warnings:

- Do not use a business name publicly during sole proprietor phase unless DBA/FBN rules are satisfied.
- During Phase 0, StartupFiles' footer/legal operator should be Edward Lee, not Whale Tales Labs.
- Buying a domain is not business formation.
- Buying a domain is not DBA/FBN registration.
- Buying a domain is not trademark clearance.
- Buying a domain is not liability protection.
- A sole proprietorship does not shield personal assets.
- A California LLC generally has an $800 annual tax even if it makes no money.
- Do not collect/store SSNs in the app for V1.
- Do not accept MOMO hardware preorders without product compliance and liability review.
- Do not make Trovr rewards redeemable without payout/tax/fraud/payment compliance review.
